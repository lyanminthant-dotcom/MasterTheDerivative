Introduction to Derivatives

In this comprehensive lecture, we explore the fundamental concepts of derivatives, starting with the definition and geometric interpretation. We'll cover:


• Limit Definition of the Derivative
   • What limits represents
   • How to evalute them
   • one-sided limits and infinite limits
   • What it means when a limit does not exist

• Continuity of Function
   • The 3 conditions for continuity
   • Types of discontinuities (jump, infinite, removable)
   • How limits predict wherther a function is continuous

• Differentiability of Function
   • What differentiate means
   • The relationship between continuity and differentiability
   • Sharp corners, cusps, vertical tangents
   • How to check if a function is differentiable at a point

• Basic Differentiatioin Rules
   • Slope of tangent
   • First Principle of Definition of Derivative
   • Constant Rule
   • Power Rule
   • Constant Multiple Rule
   • Sum & Difference Rule
   • Product Rule
   • Quotient Rule
   • Chain Rule
   • Derivatives of exponential, logarithmic, and trigonometric functions

• Application of Derivatives
   • Finding slope of tangent line
   • Rate of change
   • Displacement, Velocity, Acceleration
   • Business Application (Revenue, Price)


This lecture is designed for students beginning their journey in calculus and provides a solid foundation for more advanced topics.
